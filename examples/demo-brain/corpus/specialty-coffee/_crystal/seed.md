Great coffee comes from controlling extraction, not chasing gear. Start with
fresh beans, a repeatable ratio, and a consistent grind. If the cup tastes
sharp or thin, grind finer or increase contact time; if it tastes bitter or
muddy, grind coarser or shorten contact time. Brew one change at a time.
Consistency beats complexity. A good recipe balances sweetness, clarity, and
body while staying easy to repeat.
