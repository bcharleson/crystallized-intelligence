# Specialty Coffee — Principles

## Core Framework

Good brewing is the interaction of dose, grind size, water, time, and agitation.
Treat recipes as control systems, not magic numbers.

## Key Rules

- Use a scale for both coffee and water.
- Change one variable at a time when dialing in.
- Grind size is usually the fastest correction lever.
- Water that is too hot can exaggerate bitterness.
- Freshness matters, but repeatability matters more once beans are open.

## Common Mistakes

1. Changing multiple variables at once and losing the signal.
2. Grinding by eye instead of by outcome.
3. Assuming expensive gear fixes bad technique.
4. Ignoring brew notes and repeating avoidable errors.

## Decision Tree

- Sour and watery: grind finer or brew slightly longer.
- Bitter and dry: grind coarser or reduce extraction time.
- Flat and dull: check bean freshness and water temperature.

## Cross-Domain Links

- Habit design: good brewing improves through repeatable routines.
- Measurement: scales and simple logs beat intuition when learning.
