# Dialing In Basics

Dialing in means adjusting a brew recipe until the cup tastes balanced and
repeatable. Start with a fixed dose and brew ratio. Brew one cup, taste it, and
describe the result in simple words such as sour, bitter, thin, sweet, or
heavy. Then change a single variable.

For most brew methods, grind size is the first adjustment. Finer grinds usually
extract more; coarser grinds usually extract less. Brew time and agitation are
secondary levers. The goal is not perfect numbers. The goal is a recipe you can
repeat tomorrow and improve with confidence.
